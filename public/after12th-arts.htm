Students often wonder what to do after 12th arts? There are many courses for arts students after 12th grade exam. From creative degrees to high-paying roles in public and private sectors, the path ahead can be both rewarding and diverse. Whether it is govt coursesafter 12th arts or professional degrees in design, teaching, or business, choosing the right course plays a big role in shaping a successful future.

This blog provides a detailed list of coursesfor arts students after 12th.

GraduationCourses After 12th Arts

Graduates from the Arts stream can pursue arange of courses after 12th arts with high salary. These courses offerpotential career options after 12th arts.

Bachelor of Arts (BA)

BA is one of the most popular undergraduate degrees after 12th arts. Some popular BA specialisations include:

  • English Literature -The course includes drama, fiction, poetry and prose.
  • Sociology -Sociology revolves around human society, social phenomena, and structures.
  • Psychology -The subject focuses on studying human behaviour, mental processes and emotions.
  • Political Science -It analyses thepolitical systems, government policies and institutions. Students also study political ideologies and international political relations.
  • History -History explores historical events, civilisations, and the various socio-political developments and revolutions.

Bachelor of Commerce (BCom)

BCom is an undergraduate programme focusing on different aspects of business, commerce, and finance. Although BCom is often pursued by Commerce students, it is also open to Arts studentsinterested in business and finance careers. The main subjects of BCom includeaccounting, economics, business law, taxation, finance, marketing, andmanagement.

Bachelor of Design (BDes)

BDes(Bachelor of Design) is an undergraduate degree that helps students understand design basics and apply creative thinking to real-world challenges. Some of the popular specialisations include:

  • Fashion Design - This course teaches students to design clothes, create fashion accessories, and work with different types of fabrics and materials.
  • Graphic Design - Students learn to make visual content such as posters, logos, websites, and social media graphics.
  • Interior Design - This course covers how to plan and decorate indoor spaces, including homes, offices, shops,and other buildings.
  • Product Design - Students are trained to design and develop everyday items such as furniture, kitchen tools, and electronic gadgets that people use regularly.

Bachelor of Journalism and Mass Communication(BJMC)

BJMC is an exciting undergraduate programme combining theoretical knowledge with practical journalism and masscommunication skills. The BJM Ccurriculum covers news writing, reporting, editing, mediaethics, communication theory, etc.

Bachelor of Education (BEd)

BEd (Bachelor of Education) is one of the bestcourses after 12th arts that trains students to become professional teachers and work in the education sector. The course helps students learn how to teach,manage a classroom, and understand how children learn at different stages. Italso covers topics like lesson planning, student behaviour, and how to design learning material for different age groups. After completing BEd, students canwork as teachers in schools or take up roles like education consultants oracademic coordinators. This degree builds strong teaching skills while giving students hands-on training through internships or teaching practice. Manycolleges also support students with placements or school tie-ups, making iteasier to start a teaching career right after graduation.

Professional Courses After 12th Arts

For students looking for career options after 12th arts, there are multiple programmes that combine industry-relevant skills with strong career outcomes. Some of the courses after 12th arts with high salary are listed below:

  • Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA)
    BBA, one of the best courses after 12th arts, teaches the basics of running abusiness, from marketing and HR to finance and operations. It prepares studentsfor roles in corporate firms, banks, or startups. A BBA can also be a solid foundation for an MBA later.
  • Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM)
    BHM trains students in hospitality, including hotel services, event planning,and food management. It combines classroom learning with industry exposure through internships. Graduates often find jobsin hotels, airlines, resorts, and tourism.
  • Bachelor of Mass Communication (BMC)
    This programme covers journalism, media production, and advertising. Students learn how the media works across print, TV, digital, and social platforms. It opens doors to careers in newsrooms, Public Relations firms, ad agencies, and film houses.
  • Bachelor of Social Work (BSW)
    BSW is meant for those who want to work in communities and help people in need.It includes training in counselling, policy, and outreach work. Graduates can join NGOs, social service centres, and government programmes.

Government Competitive Exams After 12th Arts

Arts students can also prepare for several competitive exams that lead to government careers in administration, defence, law, and design:

  • CUET(Common University Entrance Test): A national-level entrancetest for admission to undergraduate courses in central and other participating universities.
  • CLAT(Common Law Admission Test): Required for admission to undergraduate law programmes at National Law Universities across India.
  • NIFT(National Institute of Fashion Technology Entrance Exam): Conducted for entry into fashion design and technology courses at NIFT campuses.
  • NID(National Institute of Design Admission Test): Forstudents aiming to pursue design programmes at top NID institutes.
  • UPSCNDA (Union Public Service Commission National Defence Academy Exam): Offers entry into the Indian Army, Navy, or Air Force through the NDA route.
  • SSC Exams (Staff Selection Commission): Includes exams like CHSL and MTS for government departments' clerical, assistant, andsupport roles.
  • State PSC Exams (State Public Service Commission): Held by individual states to recruit candidates for administrative and civil serviceroles.

High Salary Courses After 12th Arts Stream

Several professional courses after the 12th arts stream offer excellent earning potential and opportunities in creative and fast-growing industries. Students can check out the after 12th arts courses list given below

  • Graphic Design
    The course teaches how to create visuals for digital platforms, advertising, and branding.
    Students can work as graphic designers, art directors, web designers, orcreative leads.
  • Fashion Design
    This course covers clothing design, textiles, and fashion business fundamentals.
    Students can work as fashion designers, illustrators, merchandisers, or fashion stylists.
  • Digital Marketing
    The course includes SEO, content creation, online campaigns, and social medias trategy.
    Students can become digital marketers, SEO executives, social media managers, or content strategists.
  • Photography
    Students learn techniques in digital and film photography, as well as editingand composition. After the course, they can pursue job roles of photographers, photo editors, or visual storytellers.
  • Interior Design
    This course involves planning and designing interior spaces for beauty and function.
    Career options include interior designer, furniture designer, or space planner.
  • Film Making
    Students are trained in all aspects of film production, including direction, editing, and cinematography. Job roles include film director, cinematographer, or film editor.
  • Event Management
    Covers the planning, budgeting, and execution of events across different sectors.
    Students can work as event planners, wedding coordinators, or corporate eventmanagers.
  • Journalism
    The course focuses on reporting, writing, editing, and media ethics across various platforms.
    Career paths include journalist, news reporter, or content writer.
  • Animation
    Students learn 2D and 3D animation techniques used in film, games, and advertising.
    They can work as animators, VFX artists, or game designers.

Management Courses After 12th Arts

Management courses open up strong career pathsin various fields like business, travel, events, and retail. Some of therewarding options include:

  • Bachelor of Business Administration - Covers business operations, finance, marketing, HR, and more. Thecourse builds a strong base for company, bank, orstartup roles.
  • Bachelor of Management Studies - Focuses on business planning, leadership, and ethics. Suitable for careers in business management, consulting, or entrepreneurship.
  • Bachelor of Retail Management -Teaches store management, merchandising, and customer handling. Graduates often work inmalls, retail chains, or e-commerce.
  • Bachelor of Tourism and Travel Management - Deals with managing tours, hospitality, and travel services. Leadsto jobs in travel agencies, hotels, cruise lines, or tourism boards.
  • Bachelor of Event Management - Covers planning and handling events like weddings, corporate meetings, and festivals. Useful for roles in event firms or freelance projects.

Teaching Courses After 12th Arts

Various teaching courses are listed below:

  • Diploma in Elementary Education -Designed for primary school teaching, the curriculum includes child psychology, classroom methods, and curriculum design. It leads to teaching jobs in government and private schools.
  • Bachelor of Education -As discussed above, is one of the teaching courses that students can pursue after 12th.
  • Bachelor of Physical Education -Coversfitness, sports science, and coaching techniques. The course is ideal for careers in physical education, sports training, or school sportsmanagement.
  • Bachelor of Special Education -Prepares educators to support students with special needs. Focus areas include inclusive teaching, assistive tools, and behavioural strategies.

Architecture Courses After 12th Arts

Arts students interested in architecture courses after 12th can consider the following choices:

  • Bachelor of Architecture -A five-year course covering design studios, construction methods, andenvironmental planning. Graduates can work as architects, designers, or project managers in the private and public sectors.
  • Bachelor of Interior Design -Focuseson aesthetics, functionality, and spatial planning within interiors. Careeroptions include interior designer, space planner, or visual merchandiser.
  • Bachelor of Landscape Architecture -Combinesnature design with thorough landscape planning, ecology, and horticulture. Leads toroles such as landscape architect or green space designer.
  • Bachelor of Urban Planning -Centreson city development, infrastructure, and community planning. Graduates often work as urban planners, policy advisors, or transport consultants.
  • Bachelor of Construction Management -Coversproject handling, budgeting, contracts, and construction laws. Career paths include construction manager, site supervisor, or cost estimator.

Note: Some architecture programmes may requirespecific subject combinations, such as Mathematics or Science in 10+2.

Conclusion

Today, Arts students have more career options than ever before. With the right course and a clear focus, rewarding roles in design, teaching, management, or public service are well within reach. It's all about finding the right fit and moving forward with confidence.

FAQs

Q1: Whatcan I do after 12th Arts?

A1: You can choose from degree courses like BA, BBA, BFA, BSW, and professional options like design, journalism, event management, or digital marketing.

Q2: Whatare the competitive exams after 12th Arts?

A2: Here are some popular competitive exams that Arts students can appear for:

  • CUET - Common University Entrance Test
  • CLAT - Common Law Admission Test
  • NIFT - National Institute of Fashion Technology Entrance Exam
  • NID - National Institute of Design Admission Test
  • UPSC - Union Public Service Commission National Defence Academy Exam
  • SSC Exams - Staff Selection Commission Exams (like CHSL,MTS, etc.)
  • State PSC Exams - State Public Service Commission Exams forvarious administrative posts

Q3: Can Ido BBA after 12th Arts without Maths?

A3: Yes, many universities allowArts students without Maths to apply for BBA, especially under the general ormanagement quota.

Q4: Can Ido MBA after 12th Arts?

A4: Yes, you can do an MBA, but an undergraduate degree is required first; after completing graduation, students can pursue an MBA through exams like CAT, MAT, or XAT.

Q5: Can anArts student get a government job?

A5: Absolutely. Arts students can appear for exams like UPSC, SSC, state PSCs, defence services, and more for various government roles.
